Poor Cognitive Skills

Children need stimulation and quality education in early years to develop cognitive abilities. Depriving children of this can impair skills like memory, problem-solving, decision making which are critical for academic and life success. These effects may persist for lifetime.

Social & Emotional Issues

Development of social skills allows children to form relationships, communicate and collaborate effectively. Inadequate education leaves children unable to regulate their emotions, behavior or understand social norms. This leads to anti-social tendencies and inability to integrate in society.

Behavioral Problems

Children who lack proper education outlets may exhibit their distress through challenging physical and verbal behaviors. These include tantrums, fighting with peers, disobedience and abuse. Such problematic behaviors become barriers to leading a normal student life.

Physical Health Effects

Educational deprivation limits opportunities for play, interaction and stimulation - critical for physical development and health. It may manifest as growth retardation, decreased immunity or chronic issues like vitamin deficiencies affecting child's physiological thriving.
